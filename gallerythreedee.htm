Here is a gallery of some of the gadgets I've made in 3D-Desmos.
Press the down arrow in each window to hide the toolbar. I haven't figured out how to get to minimize upon loading yet.
Regulus on a Hyperboloid

A quadric surface (for example, a hyperboloid) supports two reguli: a family of disjoint lines that cover the quadric surface!


The "Mysterious Configuration"

This is a (10,12)-geproci configuration of 120 points in P^3_C, as explored in this paper by Chiantini, Farnik, Favacchio, Harbourne, Migliore, Szemberg, and Szpond. All the points of this configuration are real, but 21 are at the plane at infinity, so only 99 are visible. This is a (120_4,80_6)-configuration, but 4 of the 80 lines are in the plane at infinity, so only 76 are visible.


12 Lines on the Clebsch Cubic

A smooth cubic surface in P^3_C will have 27 lines, but not all of them will be real! This portrays the 12 real lines of the Clebsch cubic surface in A^3_R defined by the polynomial x^3+y^3+z^3+1=(x+y+z+1)^3. The Clebsch surface is special because it contains the maximal number of Eckardt points (points where three lines on the surface meet) of 10. Although, only four of them are real.
